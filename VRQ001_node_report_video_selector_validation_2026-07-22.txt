Engineering Study Node Report

Task ID: VRQ001

Title: Video Validation of Recognition_ID-Based Camera Target Selection
Project Area: Camera target-reference reconstruction and camera-radar discrepancy localization
Status: COMPLETE - Recognition_ID active-target selector hypothesis REJECTED


1. Executive Outcome

VRQ001 performed structured video review of a deterministic queue generated
from the MCRS001 Recognition_ID-referenced frame dataset.

After removal of nine pre-driving, close-range cases, 18 review records remained.
The review produced:

- Selector supported: 4 records
- Selector contradicted: 12 records
- Insufficient evidence: 2 records

The two insufficient-evidence records were beyond the available video end at
FrameCount 57597. Among the 16 reviewable records, 75 percent contradicted the
Recognition_ID active-target-selector hypothesis.

Camera tracking was labeled stable in 15 of 18 records, unstable in one, and
unreviewable/unclear in two. Many contradictions therefore occurred while the
visible/AKBPTS Camera target remained stably tracked.

Final decision:

    REJECT
    Recognition_ID alone identifies the current active Camera/AKBPTS target.

The decimal values of Recognition_ID still map to Camera CAN message IDs. The
rejected part is the semantic assumption that the referenced message is the
target currently displayed, tracked, or used for the intended preceding-target
Camera-Radar comparison.

Baseline, residual, threshold, and event modeling are paused until the true
Camera target-selection semantics are identified.


2. Review Scope and Boundary Correction

The first generated queue included cases beginning near FrameCount 6500.
Manual review showed that the vehicle did not enter the intended on-road
operating region until approximately FrameCount 7329.

Frames before 7329 contained:

- very close range;
- only a partial vehicle body in view;
- one physical vehicle represented by multiple Camera candidates or parts;
- cross traffic, motorcycles, pedestrians, walls, trees, and slopes;
- frequent Recognition_ID changes not corresponding to the intended on-road
  preceding target.

The formal main-analysis boundary is therefore modified to:

    FrameCount < 7329: PRE_VALID / OUT_OF_SCOPE_PRE_DRIVE
    FrameCount >= 7329: intended on-road analysis region
    FrameCount >= 38239: investigation region

This is a project operational-domain rule, not a universal claim that partial
vehicle-body or close-range scenes are unusable by the sensor. Those scenes may
be retained later as a separate stress-scenario study.

The original first nine queue rows were removed. The remaining rows were
renumbered Q001 through Q018 before final label encoding.


3. Review and Return Method

Each review record contained:

- WindowStartFrame
- KeyFrame
- WindowEndFrame
- ReasonCodes
- Recognition_ID-referenced Camera CAN ID

The reviewer inspected video/StcamViewer around each KeyFrame and labeled:

- CameraTargetVisible
- SelectedCameraMatchesDisplayedTarget
- VisibleTargetSwitchOrJump

RadarMeasurementPlausible was intentionally optional and was not required by
the label encoder.

The completed 18-row CSV was encoded by VRQ001 Label Encoder v0.2. The encoder
returned review labels, KeyFrame values, and ReasonCodes while leaving the CSV
unchanged.

- no row remained unreviewed;

6. Episode-Level Interpretation

The video was observed at approximately 20 frames per second. The queue had
over-sampled some events because exact KeyFrame deduplication did not merge
overlapping windows.

The corrected future rule is:

    KeyFrame difference <= 100 frames
    OR review windows overlap
    -> one Review Episode

Under the 100-frame rule:

- Q007 and Q008 form one supported episode.
- Q012 and Q013 form one mixed episode.
- Q014 and Q015 form one contradicted episode.
- Other records remain separate under the strict 100-frame threshold.

This yields 15 episodes:

- 13 reviewable episodes
- 2 unreviewable episodes beyond the video end

Among the 13 reviewable episodes:

- Supported: 2
- Contradicted: 10
- Mixed: 1

The episode-level result remains strongly inconsistent with the
Recognition_ID active-target-selector hypothesis.

If an episode containing any explicit contradiction is counted as not passing
the selector test, 11 of 13 reviewable episodes contain contradiction evidence.
This 84.6 percent is a validation-sample result, not a natural failure rate,
because the queue was deliberately enriched.


7. Engineering Observations

1. Recognition_ID-referenced Camera sources frequently differed from the
   stable Camera/AKBPTS target visible in video.

2. Wrong referenced candidates included slopes, trees, a pole, no-target
   states, and other objects while Camera 3 remained stable on the intended
   vehicle.

3. Several contradictions occurred without any visible target switch.

4. The disagreement was not limited to Recognition transitions or conflict
   frames. It also appeared in controls, Camera-ID coverage cases, discrepancy
   tails, signal-age cases, and a selection-status exception.

5. Some large positive discrepancy cases were consistent with Camera tracking
   while Radar appeared to measure the next vehicle. This supports Radar target
   mismatch as a separate hypothesis.

6. The value 327.66 was manually identified as a default no-target Camera value
   and must be added to the provisional invalid-sentinel registry, subject to
   exact signal/unit confirmation.

7. Radar field of view may be narrow enough to lose the target when the vehicle
   or target is approximately 10 to 15 degrees off the centerline. This is an
   observation/hypothesis, not a confirmed Radar specification.

8. Apparent CAN/video disagreement may arise from signal semantics, intra-frame
   ordering, P002 forward fill, update timing, or synchronization. The current
   review does not distinguish among these causes.


8. Decisions

KEEP

- FrameCount as the only currently trusted video/CAN alignment axis.
- Decimal Recognition_ID to Camera CAN-ID mapping as a representation fact.
- Recognition_ID as diagnostic state/context information.
- MessageCounter-based update reconstruction.
- SignalAgeFrames and explicit freshness provenance.
- One canonical record per FrameCount.
- Explicit missing, unsupported, invalid, conflict, and stale states.
- Radar as a provisional reference rather than ground truth.
- Video review before promoting a selector or anomaly definition.

REJECT

- Recognition_ID alone as the active Camera/AKBPTS target selector.
- Fields named SelectedCamera as approved target measurements under the current
  logic.
- Current ProvisionalDiscrepancy as input to baseline, threshold, or event
  analysis.
- Treating 6500 as the formal on-road valid-start boundary.
- Treating a large Camera-Radar discrepancy as Camera failure.
- Treating a Recognition transition as proof of a visible target switch.
- Exact-KeyFrame-only deduplication for video review cases.

MODIFY

- Rename or interpret current SelectedCamera fields as
  RecognitionReferencedCamera fields.
- Set the first main-analysis on-road boundary to approximately FrameCount 7329.
- Add 327.66 to the provisional no-target/invalid-value investigation list.
- Merge future review candidates into episodes when centers differ by no more
  than 100 frames or their review windows overlap.
- Treat video end FrameCount 57597 as the current review-availability boundary.
- Investigate AKBPTS/displayed-control-target semantics before constructing a
  new Camera target selector.
- Revalidate Maximum SignalAgeFrames = 1 after the true target signal is known;
  the present result is confounded by target-selection failure.


9. Limitations

1. The review used one video/log.
2. The queue was deliberately enriched and cannot estimate natural failure
   prevalence or false-positive rate.
3. Nine pre-driving cases were removed manually and the remaining ReviewIDs
   were renumbered.
4. The available video ended at FrameCount 57597, leaving two cases
   unreviewable.
5. Some human memos describe suspected synchronization, bus, or Radar behavior;
   those are hypotheses, not confirmed causes.
6. AKBPTS exact signal definition and relationship to CAN messages are not yet
   documented in the project state.
7. The 327.66 sentinel requires exact DBC signal and unit confirmation before
   becoming a universal validity rule.
8. The approximately 20 fps rate and 100-frame episode rule are current
   engineering assumptions and should be confirmed from video metadata.
9. Camera-Radar physical-target identity is still not directly available.
10. No baseline or residual conclusion from the rejected selector may be
    promoted to the formal pipeline.


10. Handoff to the Next Task

The next task should be a focused Reference Target Semantics investigation,
not baseline modeling.

Proposed task objective:

    Identify which available signal or state corresponds to the Camera target
    displayed/used by AKBPTS, and distinguish it from Recognition_ID.

The investigation should compare, at minimum:

- AKBPTS displayed/controlled target behavior from video;
- Recognition_ID;
- all seven Camera candidate states;
- MessageCounter update timing;
- SwitchingFlag;
- ObjectDetectionType and validity values;
- 0x5E1 states such as f_accsyssuspcanbuf, VehicleDetect, BarDisplay,
  VehicleSymbolDisplay, AccSystemStatus, and related ACC display/state fields;
- FrameCount and within-frame ordering;
- explicit invalid values including 327.66;
- selected contradiction frames from VRQ001.

Only after a replacement selector is independently video-validated may the
project resume Camera-Radar baseline, residual, threshold, persistence, and
event analysis.
